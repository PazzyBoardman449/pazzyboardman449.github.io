Scientific Research

As an applied mathematician, I spend my time researching and modelling the climate in order to gain insights into its properties and dynamics, as well as how humans are currently inlfuencing it. I am especially interested in how we can use the data produced by state-of-the-art climate models models in order to gain valuable insight into the future of the climate. My current PhD project is focussed on this area; aiming to reduce this uncertainty by uncovering emergent constraints on the evolution of our climate.

Refractive index extraction from THz radiation using peak finding
Fitting complex refractive index data to permittivity mixing models
Using image analysis to extract the contours of red blood cells (Masters Project Dissertation)
Solving differential equations to predict viscoelastic behaviour of hydrogels
Modelling coarse grained diffusion to predict morphogenesis
Solving differential equations to predict the dynamics of biochemical oscillators
